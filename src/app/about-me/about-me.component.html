About Me

I am an astronomer specializing in the evolution of planets and stars. My research focuses on exploring the characteristics of planets orbiting other stars and comprehending the evolution and dynamical measurements of planets and stars. I work with data from various telescopes and databases, including TESS, Gaia, ASAS-SN, SIMBAD, ETD, VSX, and VizieR. My objective is to make progress on the age-old question of whether there are planets capable of supporting life.

I have a master's degree in applied mathematics. Currently, I am seeking a PhD program to deepen my expertise in astronomy, with a focus on using new technologies and methodologies to model and analyze astronomical data. I am interested in applying artificial intelligence and machine learning to enhance data analysis in research.

Born and raised on this "pale blue dot," I see the world as part of a vast, interconnected universe that transcends borders and differences. Just as science seeks to bridge quantum mechanics and relativity, we must strive for unity in equity, inclusion, and diversity. In nature, diversity is not just a feature but a driving force behind adaptation and progress. By embracing diversity, we foster the creativity and resilience needed to push the boundaries of knowledge and human potential.
